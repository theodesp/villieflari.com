About Me

As a photographer, poet & fibre artist I make use of all three media, separately, to share my view and experiences of people as well as everyday interactions between them, and, interactions with their surroundings. As an artist my aim is to capture realities, to reflect upon them, and to essentially work through the process of being human. My inspiration also includes my own personal experiences, which I mainly express through photography and poetry. Within the field of photography, I am especially drawn towards street imagery and emotions which I encapsulate in abstract and/or figurative forms. Within my photographs, one can see multiple pieces of visual information which I hope will raise questions to the viewer.

Thessaloniki

Θεσσαλονίκη - Thessaloniki - is my home-town.

An ignota terra; an amphitheatre of old and new, crude buildings with their feet on a sea bay.
An Eastern harbour, peppered with hundreds of Greek Orthodox churches,
with even more tavernas and coffee places.
This was my launch point; a start for many journeys in my life- physical, mental, emotional.
Some short lived, some longer, and some on-going.
Many of those, particularly the ones that dictated my physical presence
in my home country were never realised.
The mere thought of them, or even their realisation not coming to head until very recently.
A necessary sad decision made for survival.

My Ireland

I still recall the day; sailing to my Ithaka,
contemplating my time.
My senses sharp,
only,
I forgot myself for a planck unit.
Enough for my weightless body
to be calmly brought on shore.
Of another island.


Carrying my depleted heart, I had a hope;
I found Djerba!
And, I would not long any more
for the wild mountains,
or the browned fields dotted with the smell of pine,
or the cricket songs
rolling on the sunburnt rocks
of my homeland.

But then again,
I saw people on this island
gazing
across the ocean.
I thought, maybe they are waiting for
outcasts to arrive?
Or maybe they are on guard,
fearful of any Vikings grawling?

But then again,
their eyes are not alert,
the lines on their foreheads
speak of melancholic thoughts instead.
Their backs are not curved,
ready to defend and attack.
They are walking upright,
with their chests and arms ready to open up.

And then, I saw;
every gaze they throw
leaves a stone upon long, weathered bridges
that tie long-lost dreams
with the ones that stayed behind.
Bridges, so inviting! Ready to be walked on
by those who breathe
faith, love and hope.

And then, I heard
the murmur of the island’s sand;
“I sing what happiness is”.
And I believed.
And I surrendered,
body and mind,
to the myriad greens on its ground
and the rainbow of the blues in its sky.

Maybe it was also the melody I heard in its people chatter.
Or the music exhumed from its homeless
as they stood below my waistline.
Or maybe its rains that visited faithfully every 3 days.
Maybe it was its sea, wild and luring, a feast of all violets and greys.
Or maybe its rivers, mixed and rocky.
Or perhaps the way its people give when they see the sufferers,
for they want them to stay and not bleed again.

Whatever it is I wake up every day and
I find myself even deeper in its arms,
and I feel softer and softer,
and I do not bat an eyelid
when I hear the children shouting
“I am the king of the world”!
For it is the king of islands as it flows on the seas,
perfect piece of land that never holds or fights only loves and gives.

I have been looking for my Ithaca,
and I found
My Ireland.

“Journeys”

Belief

It is very sudden.

Fear explodes in silence
in your bones.
Inelastic body moves like a robot,
but shows in flesh.
Eyes looking through iron,
face muscles stretched
to the limit.
A faint smile; sign that you are ready to operate.
And to defend.
Tension pain starts to pulse,
like a leaking faucet,
ion by ion.
Oblivion reigns in the brain.
Adrenaline and cortisol
pump is now unstoppable.
Cycle is on the move.
You fritter away,
unseen.

Is there a homecoming?

You still breathe.
Depletion spreads.
Relief is slowly coming out.
Eyes are now closing, to rest
for a fleeting moment.
Confusion is creeping up.
 Your desolate soul
lies flooded with
popping questions.
Why?
What did I do wrong?
Subservience emerges,
obedience rushes in.
Briefly, for
hope swamps
body and brain.
It will not happen again.
But it does.

Time freezes.

I feel tied.
And yet,
the chains chocking me are secret.
To other people.
Only I behold them
hanging out my parents' eyes,
cold as ice,
immense, stretching out miles away.
I dreamt I would float,
far off.
A buoyant astronaut exploring.
Wrath infiltrates my blood
while I am dragged back.
Alas.
Terror
is looting my freedom.
Afeared I will evaporate
if I severe this raucous umbilical cord.

When will this agonising end?

Break the cycle,
they say.
Fight back,
they say.
You are a survivor,
they say.
And they trade 15 minutes of their time for 50 years of yours.
Anger is now the new king in the mind.
Countless images of thyself
hitting and punching back rush in.
Again, again, again.
And the silent screams
materialise like gigantic stalactites
and break your core.
Am I the only one hearing these?
Motioning around,
all valves well sealed.
A porcelain face over a bundle of resentment and despair.

Militaristic way.


Modus operanti,
secrets well kept.
I have been walking bent.
Carrying the weight
of a sad look in my eyes.
Deprived of my conferments.
Oh, how
I yearn for another way.
Sometimes, it babbles in my dreams.
I see
my movements flowing in
an unbroken balance.
Gliding.
Rippling,
right from the centre of my soma.
Unfolding.

Flying.

My hands longing out,
swirling like a delicate piece of music,
dissolving the erstwhile.
Body rhythms.
And I am sailing unchained,
powered
from this fireball
strung from my heart.
It elongates me
into earth’s midst.
It absorbs
my weight, lifting me to the stars.
Ah, how hard is the tiny moment leading up
to unlock my eyelids.
I am petrified
of this liquid calmness vanishing.
Eyes now open, I feel warmth in my chest.
A soft flame is shimmering.

It is my choice.
